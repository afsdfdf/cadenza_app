Problem Statement & Market Opportunity

2.1 Problem Statement

Challenges for Artists

  • Delayed Royalty Payments: Traditional royalty distribution cycles can take 6–12 months, causing severe cash flow constraints for independent artists.
  • Limited Financing Channels: Most artists cannot access loans or advance payments due to the lack of collateralized financial infrastructure.
  • Dependency on Labels: Record labels retain significant bargaining power, limiting artists’ autonomy and revenue share.

Challenges for Investors

  • High Entry Barriers: Direct access to music copyrights is often restricted to institutional investors and major labels.
  • Illiquid Investments: Even when investments are possible, secondary market liquidity is virtually nonexistent.
  • Lack of Transparency: Royalty reporting is fragmented, making it difficult to track earnings and assess risk.

Solution & Product Overview

3.1 Core Solution

Cadenza redefines the way music copyrights are financed and monetized by leveraging blockchain, NFTs, DeFi, and smart contracts. The platform provides a comprehensive infrastructure where copyright assets can be digitized, tokenized, traded, and yield‑generating.

The solution integrates four critical pillars:

NFT Copyright Verification

  • Each music work (song, album, or royalty contract) is verified through collaboration with copyright registries and streaming APIs.
  • Metadata includes ownership details, royalty percentage, contract duration, and licensing rights.
  • A unique NFT is minted to represent the verified copyright.

Fractionalized Tokens (ERC‑20)

  • Copyright NFTs are locked into a tokenization smart contract.
  • NFTs are fractionalized into ERC‑20 tokens, allowing thousands of investors to co‑own a single asset.
  • Example: A song with USD 100,000 expected future royalties → 100,000 tokens minted (1 token = 0.001% royalty rights).

Marketplace & Liquidity Pools

  • A decentralized marketplace enables artists to raise capital upfront by selling fractions of their copyright.
  • Automated Market Makers (AMMs) ensure token liquidity and secondary market activity.
  • Cadenza will integrate with existing DeFi protocols (e.g., Uniswap V3, Curve) to bootstrap liquidity.

Automated Royalty Distribution

  • Streaming and licensing royalties flow directly into Cadenza’s Revenue Pool.
  • Smart contracts calculate proportional payouts and automatically distribute funds to token holders.
  • Investors can choose payout options in stablecoins (USDC/USDT) for reduced volatility.

Protocol & Technical Architecture

5.1 Overview

The Cadenza Protocol provides the technical foundation for transforming music copyrights into investable and tradable digital assets. Its architecture ensures trust, scalability, and automation, combining on‑chain smart contracts with off‑chain copyright verification.

Cadenza’s architecture is designed around five integrated layers:

  • Asset Onboarding Layer – Copyright verification and NFT minting.
  • Tokenization Layer – Fractionalization into fungible tokens.
  • Liquidity & Trading Layer – Marketplace and liquidity pools.
  • Royalty Distribution Layer – Automated revenue management.
  • Governance Layer – Community‑driven decision‑making.

Tokenization Layer Locks NFTs and fractionalizes them into fungible Cadenza Tokens. ERC‑20 token contracts, vesting logic, token issuance smart contracts Accessibility, low entry barrier

5.3 Smart Contract Modules

NFT Module

  • Creates one NFT per music asset.
  • Metadata includes work ID, copyright owner, royalty share, and distribution contract address.

Fractionalization Module

  • Locks the NFT in a smart contract.
  • Mints divisible ERC‑20 tokens representing fractionalized ownership.

Marketplace Module

  • Facilitates both primary sales (initial token distribution to investors) and secondary sales.
  • Integrates with AMM pools to guarantee token liquidity.

Royalty Pool Module

  • Acts as a vault for streaming and licensing revenues.
  • Distributes funds proportionally to token holders on a monthly or quarterly basis.

Governance Module

  • Allows token holders to vote on royalty distribution frequency, fee percentages, and partnership integrations.
  • DAO treasury funds ecosystem initiatives.

5.4 Workflow & Technical Process

Copyright Verification

  • Artist uploads work, platform connects with copyright registry APIs and auditors.
  • A digital certificate of verification is generated and stored on‑chain.

NFT Minting

  • Verified works are minted as unique NFTs.
  • Metadata includes: artist ID, royalty terms, streaming platform links.

Tokenization

  • NFT is locked into a Tokenization Contract.
  • ERC‑20 tokens (Cadenza Tokens) are minted for fractional ownership.

Liquidity & Trading

  • Tokens are distributed to investors via the Cadenza Marketplace.
  • Liquidity pools provide ongoing trading opportunities.

Royalty Flow

  • Streaming platforms send royalties into the Cadenza Royalty Pool (in stablecoins).
  • Oracles ensure real‑time reporting of revenue inflows.

Automated Distribution

  • Smart contracts distribute royalties proportionally to token holders’ balances.
  • Investors can withdraw earnings in stablecoins directly to their wallets.

Governance Participation

  • Token holders can vote on governance matters.
  • DAO ensures community‑driven growth.

5.5 Security & Compliance Features

  • Smart Contract Audits: All contracts undergo third‑party audits before deployment.
  • Data Immutability: Copyright data anchored on‑chain with IPFS hash storage.
  • Cross‑Chain Compatibility: Supports Ethereum mainnet, BSC, and Layer‑2 solutions for scalability.
  • Oracles: Trusted data feeds connect real‑world streaming revenue with on‑chain distribution contracts.

5.6 Scalability & Future Expansion

6.2 Compliance Framework

The music copyright industry is tightly regulated. Cadenza integrates compliance mechanisms to align with international copyright laws and financial regulations:

Copyright Law Alignment

  • Collaborations with copyright registries (e.g., ASCAP, BMI, PRS, international equivalents).
  • Legal anchoring of NFT metadata to ensure enforceability in real‑world disputes.

Securities Regulation

  • Token design emphasizes utility + royalty rights rather than speculative securities.
  • Jurisdictional legal reviews conducted before major token launches.

KYC/AML Policies

  • Artist onboarding includes identity verification and ownership proof.
  • Investor compliance modules for anti‑money laundering standards.

Tax & Reporting

  • Smart contracts provide transparent royalty distribution records.
  • Potential integration with tax reporting APIs for investors and artists.

6.3 Governance

The governance model of Cadenza is structured as a DAO (Decentralized Autonomous Organization), ensuring that major protocol decisions are transparent, inclusive, and community‑driven.

Governance Structure

  • Proposal Stage – Any token holder with a minimum threshold (e.g., 0.5% of circulating tokens) can submit proposals.
  • Discussion Stage – Proposals are discussed within community forums and governance platforms.
  • Voting Stage – Token holders vote proportionally to their holdings.
  • Execution Stage – Approved proposals are executed via on‑chain governance contracts.

Governance Scope Includes

  • Protocol upgrades (smart contract changes, new modules).
  • Ecosystem fund allocation (e.g., grants, liquidity incentives).
  • Partnership approvals (streaming services, copyright agencies).
  • Adjustment of platform fees and royalty distribution schedules.

6.4 Transparency & Accountability

  • On‑Chain Dashboards: Real‑time visibility of token distribution, royalty flows, and governance decisions.
  • Audit Reports: Periodic smart contract and legal audits published openly.
  • Community Oversight: Token holders can form committees for monitoring key risk and compliance areas.
